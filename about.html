Hi, I'm Sarah!

I work where systems meet people.

With a background in data science and digital media, I like taking what feels abstract and making it understandable, whether that’s through a Python script, a rubric, or a well-placed question.

At Texas Woman’s University, I’ve supported 50+ students weekly as a graduate assistant by grading, tutoring, and redesigning assignments when needed. When rubrics didn’t exist, I made them. Alongside that, I’ve published data analysis research using Python, R, and SQL.

Before this, I worked in IT support and retail. Those roles taught me how to move quickly, think clearly, and translate between systems – digital or human.

These days, I keep returning to questions about identity, behavior, and communication. My thesis explores the intersection of gender and neurodiversity – not just as theory, but as something deeply personal. I want to study people the way I study computers: to understand what’s working, what’s breaking, and what’s worth rebuilding.
